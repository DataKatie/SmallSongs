Small Song

The reeds give
way to the

wind and give
the wind away

-- A.R. Ammons

About Small Songs

This is a journal for small poem forms including haiku, tanaka, and others. Poetry is inspired by nature. There are small songs all around us. Small songs is always accepting submissions from new and aspiring poets as well as established voices. For more information on how to submit your poetry, please visit submissions .

About Haiku

Haiku is traditional Japanese poetry form composed of 3 lines, each with a specific number of syllables. Traditionally, the first line has 5 syllables, the second line has 7 syllables, and the final line again has 5 syllables. Haiku poems are frequently about nature and rarely use similes and metaphors.

thunderless lightning
sparks in emerald valley
the dance of fireflies

-- Tamara De Dominicis

About Tanka

Tanka is another traditional Japanese poetry form. It is less well-known than haiku, and contains more lines and syllables. A tanka poem is traditionally composed of 5 lines. The pattern of syllables is 5, 7, 5, 7, 7.

a long Sunday drive
south on a winding beach road
to find my poem
I listen for profound words
from a knowing wind and sea

-- Rhonda Johnson-Saunders
